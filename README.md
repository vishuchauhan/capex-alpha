# capex-alpha
Multi-Agent Capex Intelligence Engine
CapexAlpha is a multi-agent investment intelligence system designed to detect early signals of capital expenditure (capex) across companies. In capital markets, capex decisions are among the earliest indicators of future growth, as they reflect a company’s intent to expand capacity, invest in new projects, or strengthen its competitive position. However, these signals are often fragmented across financial statements, regulatory filings, and unstructured sources such as news articles and earnings calls. This fragmentation makes timely detection both complex and inefficient for investors.

Traditional screening methods rely heavily on backward-looking financial metrics, which often capture capex only after it has already impacted financial performance. CapexAlpha addresses this gap by shifting the focus toward forward-looking indicators. It combines machine learning, AI agents, and game-theoretic reasoning to identify companies investing for future growth before it becomes visible in earnings or stock performance. The system is built on the idea that capex is not merely a financial activity but a strategic decision influenced by industry dynamics, competition, and market cycles.

At its core, CapexAlpha integrates multiple data sources, including NSE and BSE filings, SEBI disclosures, MCA records, financial statements, earnings call transcripts, and news data. Each source contributes a different dimension of insight. Structured data such as financial statements and filings provide reliability and quantitative evidence, while unstructured data such as news and management commentary offer early qualitative signals. The model is designed around the principle of data fusion, where meaningful insights emerge only when these diverse sources are analyzed together rather than in isolation.

The system operates through a multi-agent architecture consisting of five specialized agents, each responsible for a distinct analytical function. The Financial Agent evaluates whether a company has the capacity to undertake capex by analyzing metrics such as CWIP growth, capex-to-sales ratios, and debt trends. The NLP Agent processes textual data to identify expansion-related signals, such as announcements of new plants, capacity increases, or investment plans. The Industry Agent examines peer behavior within a sector to determine whether capex activity is isolated or part of a broader industry trend. The Strategy Agent builds on this by applying game-theoretic reasoning to classify whether a company’s investment behavior reflects coordinated growth within an industry or a risk-taking move in isolation. Finally, the Coordinator Agent aggregates the outputs of all agents to generate a unified capex score.

CapexAlpha defines a valid capex signal as the intersection of three components: financial strength, narrative confirmation, and strategic context. Financial strength ensures that the company has the ability to fund its investments, narrative confirmation validates intent through qualitative signals, and strategic context places the decision within the broader industry landscape. This multi-layered approach helps reduce false positives and ensures that detected signals are both credible and meaningful.

The scoring methodology is designed to reflect the relative importance of each component. The final capex score is computed as a weighted combination of the financial score, NLP score, and strategy score, with the highest weight assigned to the strategy component. This reflects the system’s emphasis on forward-looking insights, as industry dynamics and competitive behavior often provide stronger signals of future growth than standalone financial metrics. By incorporating game theory into the scoring process, the model moves beyond detection and begins to interpret the underlying intent behind investment decisions.

The system follows a structured screening pipeline that begins with data ingestion and signal extraction, followed by validation across multiple sources, industry-level analysis, and strategic classification. The final output is a ranked list of companies based on their capex scores, along with detailed explanations of why each company was flagged. This ensures that the model remains fully explainable, with every output traceable back to specific financial indicators, textual signals, and industry behavior. Unlike black-box models, CapexAlpha prioritizes transparency, making it suitable for real-world investment decision-making.

The model can be validated through backtesting against actual capex execution, revenue growth, and stock performance following detected signals. Key evaluation metrics include precision, to minimize false positives, and recall, to ensure early detection of emerging opportunities. While the system is designed to be robust, it is subject to certain limitations, including data lags in regulatory filings, noise in unstructured data sources, potential bias in management disclosures, and sensitivity to macroeconomic shocks that may impact capex decisions.

CapexAlpha has strong practical applications in investment research and portfolio management. It can be used for early-stage investment screening, identification of sectoral growth cycles, and generation of forward-looking insights for institutional research workflows. By detecting capex activity before it is fully reflected in financial performance, the system enables investors to position themselves ahead of market trends.

In essence, CapexAlpha transforms fragmented and noisy data into structured, explainable, and forward-looking investment intelligence. It represents a shift from analyzing what has already happened to anticipating what is likely to happen next, providing a more proactive approach to decision-making in capital markets.
